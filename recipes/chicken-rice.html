Chicken Rice

Description

A beloved national staple featuring poached or roasted succulent chicken, served with intensely flavorful rice cooked in chicken fat and broth, accompanied by a tangy chili sauce and a light cucumber garnish.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1.5kg)
  • 4-inch piece of ginger (sliced into thick coins)
  • 2 bundles of pandan leaves (tied into knots)
  • 6 cloves garlic (peeled and lightly bruised)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 cups long-grain jasmine rice (washed and drained)
  • 2 tbsp chicken fat or butter
  • 1 stalk lemongrass (bruised)
  • 5 fresh red chilies
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Poach the Chicken: Rub the chicken generously with salt inside and out, then rinse. In a large pot of boiling water, add half of the ginger, half of the garlic, and one pandan leaf bundle. Submerge the chicken, lower the heat, and simmer gently for 30 minutes until cooked through.
  2. Ice Bath and Glaze: Remove the chicken from the pot (save the chicken broth for later!). Immediately submerge the chicken in a bowl of ice water for 5 minutes to get a jelly-like skin texture. Drain, then brush the skin with a mix of sesame oil and light soy sauce. Cut into serving pieces.
  3. Cook the Rice: Heat chicken fat or butter in a pan and sauté the remaining garlic and ginger until fragrant. Add the washed rice and stir-fry for 2 minutes. Transfer everything to a rice cooker, add the remaining pandan leaves, lemongrass, a pinch of salt, and fill with the reserved chicken broth to the required level. Cook as normal.
  4. Make the Chili Sauce: Blend the red chilies, a small piece of ginger, a clove of garlic, lime juice, sugar, a pinch of salt, and 3 tablespoons of the hot chicken broth together until smooth.
  5. Assemble: Serve a scoop of the aromatic rice alongside the chicken pieces. Garnish with cucumber slices and serve with the chili sauce and a small bowl of the remaining chicken broth as a side soup.
