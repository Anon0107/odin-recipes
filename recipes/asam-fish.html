Asam Fish

Description

A classic Malaysian sour and spicy fish stew, simmered in a vibrant, aromatic tamarind broth with lady's fingers and tomatoes.

Ingredients

  • Fish: 500g firm white fish steaks or whole fish (such as mackerel, stingray, or red snapper)
  • 2 stalks lemongrass (white parts only)
  • 4-5 shallots
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1-inch ginger
  • 1-inch galangal
  • 1-inch fresh turmeric
  • 10-15 dried chilies (soaked in hot water and deseeded)
  • 1 tsp toasted shrimp paste (belacan)
  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste, mixed with 3 cups of water and strained for juice
  • 1 wild ginger flower (bunga kantan), halved lengthwise
  • 1 sprig Vietnamese coriander (daun kesum)
  • 6 lady's fingers
  • 1 tomato, cut into wedges
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Blend the Paste: Grind the lemongrass, shallots, garlic, ginger, galangal, turmeric, dried chilies, and shrimp paste together until a smooth paste forms.
  2. Sauté: Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Fry the blended paste for 5 to 7 minutes until fragrant and the oil starts to separate.
  3. Simmer the Broth: Pour in the strained tamarind water. Add the wild ginger flower and Vietnamese coriander. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Cook Vegetables and Fish: Add the lady's fingers and fish slices. Cover and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
  5. Finish and Serve: Gently stir in the tomato wedges. Season with salt and sugar to taste, then serve hot with steamed white rice.
